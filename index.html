Rules of Hexor

Hexor is a two-player game. The board is empty at the onset of the game. Green makes the first move, after which Yellow and Green alternate until the end of the game.

Goal:

The player with most taken hexagons wins. A player get 1 point for every hexagon in their own color. If a player run out of time, the player lose immedilty. It dosent matter then who has the most hexagons.

On your turn you choose 1 of 4 options:

Expand

Build tower

Attack

Pass

Expand

Turn two empty hexagons in to a hexagon into your own color. The hexagons do not have to be placed next to each other.

Build tower

Select one of your already taken hexgons. This is now turning into a tower. The tower does not give a score, but on the other hand, the tower gives +1 in attack and defense.

Attack

Select one of the opponent's hexagons and you will attack this. In an attack, all surrounding hexagons count to the hexagon you attacked. The other player defends with all surrounding hexagons count to the hexagon you attacked.

Pass

You pass your turn. If both player pass next after each other the game is over. The player whose turn it is to move may affirm the end of a game and pass. If the other player does not wish to end the game, he may continue playing, in which case the player who passed automatically regains the right to play. Play continues until both sides agree that the game has ended.
